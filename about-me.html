  • Work:
    I am currently a Senior Quantitative Research Analyst at Graham Capital Management, L.P. With rigorous risk management and portfolio construction, we provide diversifying alternative investment strategies by developing proprietary trading signals through signal processing, statistical modeling, machine learning, and resilient execution infrastructure.

    Studies:
    I obtained my Ph.D. in physics from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) in 2022. My research was on gravitational-wave (GW) signal detection and astrophysical/cosmological inference of GW signals with interferometric detectors at the Laser Interferometer Gravitational-wave Observatory (LIGO) as a member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration. In particular, I led the algorithmic development of an end-to-end data pipeline to map out the stochastic GW background intensity on the sky using various statistical methods. Also, along with my colleagues, I developed and operated an analysis pipeline to identify and characterize weak GW signals in noisy data through matched filtering. My thesis advisor was Prof. Alan J. Weinstein.
