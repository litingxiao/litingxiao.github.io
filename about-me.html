  • Work:

    I am a PhD candidate in physics at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech). My advisor is Prof. Alan J. Weinstein.

    I work on the signal detection and data analysis of gravitational-wave signals with interferometric detectors in the Laser Interferometer Gravitational-wave Observatory (LIGO). In particular, I study the astrophysical properties of black holes using detected gravitational waves through computational approaches such as signal processing, statistical inference and machine learning. I am a member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration.

    Besides physics and astronomy, I am an enthusiast about computational science and am pursuing a Minor in Computational Science and Engineering at Caltech. I have applied machine learning techniques not just to conduct research, but also to solve hard but interesting problems.
    [Read more about my fun AI projects.]

    I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Astronomy-Physics with High Distinctions from the University of Virginia (UVA) in 2015. I was also an Echols Scholar at UVA College of Arts & Sciences and a member of Sigma Pi Sigma - American physics honor society.
    The top three undergrad research projects I was deeply involved in include searching for dark matter indirectly in the NOvA Collaboration at Fermilab, improving gravitational-wave searches at Caltech LIGO as a SURF student, and measuring cosmic ray doses at commercial flight altitudes in the UVA JefferSat Cosmic Ray Mission funded by NASA.
