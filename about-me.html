  • Physics:

    I am a PhD student in physics at the California Institute of Technology. My advisor is Prof. Alan J. Weinstein.

    I work on the detection and analysis of gravitational waves with interferometric detectors such as the Advanced Laser Interferometer Gravitational-wave Observatory (aLIGO). In particular, I study the astrophysics of black holes, the black hole population in the Universe, and the cosmological parameters using detected gravitational waves. I am a member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration.
    [Read more about gravitational waves . Read more about my research.]

    I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Astronomy-Physics with High Distinctions from the University of Virginia in 2015. I was also an Echols Scholar at UVA College of Arts & Sciences and a member of Sigma Pi Sigma - American physics honor society.
    The top three undergrad research projects I was deeply involved in include searching for dark matter indirectly in the NOvA Collaboration at Fermilab, improving gravitational-wave searches at Caltech LIGO as a SURF student, and measuring cosmic ray doses at commercial flight altitudes in the UVA JefferSat Cosmic Ray Mission funded by NASA.


    Life:

    Beyond physics, I enjoy traveling around the world. I've set foot in 22 countries as of date, and still counting. One goal of mine is "30 before 30" (visit 30 countries before turning 30)! I am also a foodie and an art museum/gallery lover.
